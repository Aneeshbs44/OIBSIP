Early Life

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. He was the youngest of three siblings and grew up in Uttam Nagar, a suburb of Delhi. Kohli's father, Prem Kohli, was a criminal lawyer and his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a housewife.

As a child, Kohli was passionate about cricket and would often spend hours playing with his brother and friends. He joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy when he was nine years old and began to hone his skills.

Career

Kohli made his international debut for India in August 2008, in an ODI against Sri Lanka. He scored his first century in February 2009, in an ODI against Sri Lanka. Since then, he has become one of the best batsmen in the world and has scored over 20,000 runs in international cricket.

Kohli has also been the captain of the Indian cricket team since 2017 and has led the team to many victories, including a historic series win against Australia in 2020-21.

Achievements

  • ICC Cricketer of the Year: 2017, 2018
  • Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World: 2016, 2017, 2018
  • Padma Shri: 2017
  • Arjuna Award: 2013
  • Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna: 2018
